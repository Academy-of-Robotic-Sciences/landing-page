Welcome to Robot Campus - Your Journey Starts Here

January 15, 2024 ¬∑ 4 min read
Robot Campus Contributors
ARS Faculty
Dr. Sarah Chen
Lead Instructor - Robotic Design

We're thrilled to announce the official launch of Robot Campus - your gateway to mastering humanoid robotics through hands-on, project-based learning. Whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced maker, we have a path designed for you.

Our Mission‚Äã

Robot Campus exists to democratize robotics education. We believe that the future of humanoid robotics shouldn't be limited to elite institutions or well-funded labs. Through open-source materials, affordable hardware, and a supportive global community, we're making advanced robotics accessible to everyone.

What Makes Us Different?‚Äã

Unlike purely theoretical courses, Robot Campus emphasizes building actual robots. Our flagship project, the SO-100/SO-101 robotic arm, costs less than $200 to build but teaches principles used in industrial robotics. You'll work with real sensors, actuators, and control systems - not just simulations.

üß† Cutting-Edge AI Integration‚Äã

Through our partnership with the LeRobot framework, you'll learn to train robots using the same techniques employed by leading AI labs:

  • Imitation learning
  • Reinforcement learning
  • Sim-to-real transfer
  • Computer vision integration

üåç Open Source Everything‚Äã

All our materials are open source:

  • Course content and curricula
  • Hardware designs and STL files
  • Software and code examples
  • Community projects

You're not just learning - you're contributing to a growing ecosystem.

Getting Started‚Äã

Step 1: Choose Your Track‚Äã

Review our Learning Tracks Overview to find the path that matches your interests and goals.

Step 2: Join the Community‚Äã

  • Discord: Join our server for real-time help and discussions
  • GitHub: Star our repositories and contribute
  • Forum: Engage in deeper technical discussions

Step 3: Set Up Your Workspace‚Äã

Depending on your track, you'll need:

  • A computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
  • Basic tools (varies by track)
  • Optional: 3D printer access, Arduino/Raspberry Pi

Step 4: Start Learning‚Äã

Begin with our foundation courses (RC-101 through RC-106) that all students complete, then dive into your specialized track.
