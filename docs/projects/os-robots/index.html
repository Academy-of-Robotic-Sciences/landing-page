os-robots

NameWhat it is3D-Printed / Printable Parts?Notes / Use-Case
InMoovA fully open-source humanoid robot (upper body, arms, hands, head, torso)Yes — main body components are 3D printable. (Wikipedia)Excellent for learning design + mechanical integration + control; strong community; many iterations. (Wikipedia)
Berkeley Humanoid LiteAn accessible, customizable humanoid robot with modular 3D-printed actuators & gearboxesYes — many parts and gearboxes 3D printed. (lite.berkeley-humanoid.org)Under ~$5,000 cost; good for research & sim-to-real; strong in actuator design. (lite.berkeley-humanoid.org)
NimbRo-OP2 / OP2XLarge open-source humanoid research robot (~135 cm, full-body)Yes — exoskeleton and many structural parts 3D printed. (arXiv)Good for larger scale, full environment interaction; heavy but proven. (arXiv)
ToddlerBotML-compatible humanoid robot for loco-manipulation, ~0.56m heightYes — entirely 3D-printed design and commercially available components. (arXiv)Great for AI track; scaled size, good for safety, data collection, research. (arXiv)
Kayra3D-printable humanoid, easy to modifyYes — parts (legs, servos, etc) are 3D printed and tutorials for assembly. (kayra.org)Good for EDU / maker spaces; can learn assembly, walking, printing. (kayra.org)
Project OpaNoidModular teen-/adult-sized humanoid robot for education & researchYes — chassis and many structural pieces are printed (FDM). (GitHub)Excellent modular design; good match for design/hardware tracks. (GitHub)
Zeroth-01 Bot (Zeroth Robotics/zeroth-bot)3D-printed humanoid robot platform supporting sim-to-real, vision, speechYes — printer-friendly structure; full hardware and software open source. (GitHub)Strong for AI / control / robotics integration tracks; low BOM; community work. (GitHub)
AsterVague humanoid / semi-humanoid (arms/head), movable parts via servosYes — nearly all structural parts 3D printed; uses off-the-shelf servos & Arduino. (Open-Electronics)Good warmup project; simple, visible movements; easy to reproduce. (Open-Electronics)
OpenArmOpen-source humanoid robot arm for real-world / contact-rich environmentsSome parts are 3D printed (for casing, joints), mixed with CNC or metal parts. (OpenArm)Good for control engineer & hardware specializations; more robust than pure 3D plastic in some parts. (OpenArm)
Hackberry3D-printable bionic arm (prosthetic style)Yes — full model data, PCB, code, CAD, STL etc available. (HACKberry)More for arm / hand mechanics; grips and control; great for prototyping & dexterous end effectors. (HACKberry)
Robot Nano HandA 3D-printed, tendon-driven humanoid hand + palm camera + AI gesturesYes — primarily printed fingers, palm, etc. (robotnanohand.com)Excellent for “hand / end effector / vision + control” track; compact and rich in DOFs. (robotnanohand.com)
MOTUS6-DoF open-source robotic arm, almost completely 3D printedYes — very high use of 3D printed structure. (Instructables)Good for learning arm kinematics, control; less humanoid but highly relevant. (Instructables)
Thor (AngelLM/Thor)6-DoF 3D-printed robotic arm, mid size (~625mm)Yes — uses 3D printed gears, components; open source STLs and code. (GitHub)For medium payload arm projects, mixing hardware + control; good for prototyping with performance. (GitHub)
